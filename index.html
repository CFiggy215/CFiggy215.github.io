DON’T DO EVERYTHING AT ONE TIME!

If you try to change your entire diet right out of the gate, you'll find out very quickly just how tough it is to do so. So start slow, make 1 good change a week. The biggest and best change you can make is to eat a good balance breakfast every morning. I know what you're thinking, "I don't have time" is the biggest one, the second one is "I'm not hungry in the morning" Understand, the reason you don't feel hungry in the morning, is your body is in a "fasted" state. We sleep for about 7-8 hours a night with no food, so our body has begun to break down our muscle tissue for energy. You NEED to get out of the fasted state in the morning as soon as possible. So one thing I highly recommend to do is take a Protein Shake first thing in morning! LEVEL-1 from 1stphorm is what I call, "The Breakfast Excuse Destroyer" it's one of the big tasting shakes I've ever had and it will give your body what it needs to start your day off right! Shake it; Drink it; Ready to Go!!!
We tend to eat less throughout the day and try to make up for it at dinner time. Sustaining with Lunch and Dinner is typical for most adults since they try to make up for lack of nutrition by over eating at dinner, but by adding Breakfast back into the mix for 1 full week you’ll notice more energy throughout the day. Reintroducing breakfast will create a shift in your metabolism leading to less cravings for over eating at the end of the day. Try to focus on eating more balanced meals throughout the day, a balanced meal consist of a fist sized serving of Protein, Carbohydrates and Vegetables. I will list my daily diet below so you will have an idea of what a typical meal for me looks like. You should aim to eat at least 4-5 meals a day. Eating these smaller, more frequent meals will boost your metabolism and give you more energy throughout the day as well. This is just an overview for basic dieting, if you have more specific questions, feel free to send me an email by clicking the email button at the top right of the screen!
